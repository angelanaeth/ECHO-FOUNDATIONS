Foundations Manual

The intro-level coaching playbook for new EchoDevo coaches — before you graduate to the full ECHO Manual.

Welcome

Welcome to the EchoDevo Foundations Manual.

This manual is the entry point into the EchoDevo coaching system. It exists to bring new coaches up to speed on the language, the zones, the testing protocols, the block types, and the coaching philosophy that the full ECHO Coaching Manual is built on.

Foundations is intentionally simpler than ECHO. ECHO is a complete decision-making framework — it teaches coaches how to diagnose athletes, select training blocks, dose stress, and translate decisions into clear athlete communication. Foundations teaches the vocabulary and the foundational physiology that has to be in place before any of that decision-making makes sense.

Read this manual first. Then graduate to ECHO when you're ready.

How to Use This Manual

Foundations is organized in the order a new coach should learn the system:

  • Zones — the language of intensity. HR, power, pace, RPE, and how they connect.
  • Testing — CP and CS — how we establish an athlete's actual sustainable threshold instead of guessing.
  • Block Types — the building blocks of any training plan.
  • Fueling & Hydration — the Power of 3, the sweat test, and the basics of carbohydrate.
  • Mental Foundations — the mindset skills that determine whether physical fitness translates into performance.

Each section can be read on its own, but the manual is designed to be read in order. The zones don't make sense without testing, the blocks don't make sense without zones, and the fueling and mindset sections amplify everything else.

The Two Foundational Rules

Before any of the technical content matters, two principles sit underneath all of it.

1. Personalization — Train YOUR body, not a generic plan.
Every athlete has unique physiology, life context, training history, and response to load. A workout that builds one athlete will break another. The numbers we set — heart rate zones, power zones, paces, fueling targets — are derived from that specific athlete's testing and history, not from a chart in a magazine.

2. Use data and principles to make decisions.
Coaching is not guessing. We anchor every training and pacing decision in objective signals: Critical Power, Critical Speed, heart rate response, perceived exertion, and how the athlete is actually adapting week to week. Vibes are not a plan.

These two rules pull in opposite directions on purpose. Personalization keeps us from rigid template-coaching. Data keeps us from drifting into "whatever feels right." Holding both at the same time is what separates coaching from cheerleading.

Who Foundations Is For

  • New coaches joining the EchoDevo system who need to learn the vocabulary before working with athletes.
  • Coaches who want a clean reference for explaining basic concepts to their own athletes.
  • Anyone who wants to understand how endurance training actually works before diving into the full ECHO framework.

When you can explain zones, testing, blocks, fueling, and mindset in plain language to an athlete — and the athlete walks away understanding — you're ready for ECHO.

What's Next

When you finish this manual, your next step is the full ECHO Coaching Manual — the complete decision-making framework, advanced testing protocols, the metabolic map, block selection logic, StressLogic, race execution, and the troubleshooting library. Foundations gives you the vocabulary. ECHO teaches you to coach with it.

Zones placeholder · canonical

Heart-rate zones are the foundational anchor for everything we do. The values below are pulled directly from the ECHO Manual and will always match — if a zone changes in ECHO, Foundations updates automatically.

Canonical reference (live from ECHO): these values are auto-synced. Do not edit by hand.
  • Z1 Recovery / Easy — <81% of LTHR
  • Z2 Aerobic Endurance — 81-89% of LTHR
  • Z3 Tempo — 90-93% of LTHR
  • Z4 Lactate Threshold — 94-99% of LTHR
  • Z5 VO2 / Anaerobic — 100%+ of LTHR

Block Types placeholder · canonical

Foundational block-type vocabulary. The full ECHO Manual layers in advanced variants — these intro blocks are the starting point.

Canonical reference (live from ECHO):
  • Base — Build aerobic capacity and durability. Mostly Z1-Z2 volume.
  • Build — Introduce threshold and tempo work on top of aerobic base. Z3-Z4 emphasis.
  • Peak — Race-specific intensity and durability. Mix of Z3-Z5 with race-pace work.
  • Taper — Reduce volume while preserving sharpness ahead of A-race.
  • Recovery — Active recovery week, ~50-65% normal volume, Z1-Z2 only.
